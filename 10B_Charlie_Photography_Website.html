What is Photography?

The word Photography literally means 'drawing with light', which derives from the Greek photo, meaning light and graph, meaning to draw. Photography is the process of recording an image a photograph on light-sensitive film or, in the case of digital photography, via a digital electronic or magnetic memory.

Photography: History and Evolution in the Philippines

Photography in the Philippines has evolved significantly over the years. Here is a chronological overview of its development:

1. The Beginnings (1800s)

Photography was introduced to the Philippines in the mid-1800s, soon after its invention. Early photographers used daguerreotypes (the first commercially successful photographic process) to capture images of prominent figures and landscapes.

2. Early 20th Century (1900s)

In the early 1900s, with the advent of the Kodak camera, photography became more accessible to the public. Filipino photographers began capturing the daily lives of ordinary people, turning photography into a more popular art form.

3. The Rise of Studio Photography (1920s-1950s)

During the 1920s-1950s, photography studios became widespread, and portrait photography was highly popular. The black and white photo studios were known for their precise lighting techniques and detailed compositions.

4. Post-War and Color Photography (1960s-1980s)

By the 1960s and 1980s, color photography began replacing black and white as the primary method. It allowed more vibrant images and improved the production of advertising, magazine photography, and family portraits.

5. Digital Photography Era (1990s-present)

With the advent of digital cameras and smartphones, photography became even more accessible and versatile. The digital age has revolutionized how people capture and share images, allowing for quicker, more widespread distribution.
